Louis Thayer Hills

Louis Thayer Hills (1936–2019) was a prolific Wisconsin artist whose work spanned more than six decades. Beginning in the 1950s, he produced a substantial and wide-ranging body of drawings, sketches, sculptures, paintings, and prints. This site honors his artistic legacy and shares information about an upcoming memorial exhibition and art sale, offering collectors the rare chance to acquire original works directly from the estate, with pricing structured to place the work into many hands.

About the Collection

The collection encompasses decades of work across a broad range of media, subjects, and scales. Due to the scope of the estate, this offering includes both intimate works on paper and larger statement pieces suitable for residential, commercial, and hospitality settings.

Pricing has been structured to reflect the size and breadth of the collection, with the intention of making original works accessible while respecting the artist’s lifetime of production.

Public Exhibition & Sale

The public memorial exhibition and sale will provide visitors the opportunity to view and acquire original works by Louis Thayer Hills in person. Selected works will be available for purchase throughout the exhibition period.

Dates, location, and additional details will be announced prior to the opening.

Trade & Bulk Viewing

Due to the scale of the collection, a dedicated viewing period will be offered for restaurants, hotels, interior designers, and other commercial buyers interested in acquiring multiple works.

This trade preview allows buyers to select cohesive groupings of original artworks and explore a wide range of sizes and styles well suited for larger spaces. Works are available directly from the artist’s estate, with pricing structured to support volume acquisitions.

Trade preview access is available by request. Scheduling details will be shared in advance of the event.

Pricing & Acquisition

Original works will be offered at accessible price points reflecting the size and scope of the estate. The goal of this sale is to place the work into active display — in homes, collections, and public-facing spaces — rather than storage.

Both individual collectors and buyers seeking multiple works are welcome.
