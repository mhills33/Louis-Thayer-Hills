Louis Thayer Hills

1936 – 2019

Louis Thayer Hills was a prolific Wisconsin artist whose work spanned more than six decades. Beginning in the 1950s, he produced a substantial and wide-ranging body of drawings, sketches, sculptures, paintings, and prints. This site honors his artistic legacy and shares information about an upcoming memorial exhibition and art sale, offering collectors the rare chance to acquire original works directly from the estate, with pricing structured to place the work into many hands.

About the Collection

The collection encompasses decades of abstract work across a broad range of media, subjects, and scales. From hundreds of pages of ink and marker in spiral-bound notebooks, to oversize masonite paintings 4' x 5', to stacks and stacks of etchings/prints on paper, the offerings range from intimate sketch studies to huge statement pieces.

Trade & Hospitality Viewing

Due to the scale of the collection, a dedicated viewing period will be offered for restaurants, hotels, interior designers, and other commercial buyers interested in acquiring multiple works.

This trade preview allows buyers to select cohesive groupings of original artworks and explore a wide range of sizes and styles well-suited for larger spaces. Works are available directly from the artist's estate, with pricing structured to support volume acquisitions.

Trade preview access is available by appointment on Thursday, June 4th, 2026 from 12pm-6pm

Pricing & Acquisition

Pricing has been structured to reflect the size and breadth of the collection, with the intention of making original works accessible while respecting the artist's lifetime of production. The goal of this sale is to place the work into active display — in homes, collections, and public-facing spaces — rather than storage.
