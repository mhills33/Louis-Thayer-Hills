Louis Thayer Hills

1935–2019

Biography

Louis Thayer Hills was an American artist who produced staggering numbers of paintings, drawings, and prints beginning as a young man in the 1950s and continuing until his passing in 2019 at the age of 83.

Born December 30, 1935, in Salt Lake City, Utah, Louis grew up in a Mormon family in the post-war years. After early studies at the University of Utah, he moved to Chicago, where he worked in the picture gallery department at Marshall Field’s restoring and framing artwork—an experience that deeply influenced his artistic path.

Louis later settled in southeastern Wisconsin with his wife Kay Ann Plutchak, where they raised their family and restored a farmhouse that became both home and creative retreat. While working professionally at J.I. Case, Louis traveled internationally and continued creating art relentlessly.

After retiring in 1993, his artistic output intensified dramatically, resulting in decades of prolific creation across media. Even in the final years of his life, as his health declined, Louis continued to make art daily—work that remained colorful, imaginative, and deeply human.

Louis passed away peacefully on June 26, 2019, leaving behind a vast body of work and a lasting artistic legacy.
